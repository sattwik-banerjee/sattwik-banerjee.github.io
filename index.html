01 · About Me

Who Am I?

I grew up playing a lot of sports, and the ones I loved the most were basketball and baseball. As I grew older I started paying more attention to the stats belonging to a player or team and I started to find it really interesting how numbers could tell a story especially when it came to understanding player performance and team dynamics. That curiosity about what data could reveal led me down a rabbit hole into statistics and math, and eventually into machine learning.

After I realized the potential of machine learning and its potential applications to almost any problem in any field, I was hooked. Now I'm at UCLA studying Data Theory, doing research at the Garrick Institute for Risk Sciences, and trying to find new ways to use AI and ML to solve problems that actually matter.

Outside of school you'll probably find me somewhere on a trail, in a gym, or in a car headed somewhere new. I love hiking, road trips, lifting, and picking up random new skills with my friends. I'm always looking for ways to push my limits and test myself, whether that's physically or intellectually.
